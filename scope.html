INTRODUCTION

Dementia, a neurological disease that predominantly affects individuals aged 60 and above, results in a deterioration of cognitive functions and necessitates the provision of person-centered care. Despite the loss of self-awareness experienced by dementia patients, their capacity to perceive and engage with music remains unaffected. Extensive research demonstrates the potential effectiveness of music interventions, physical activity engagements, and brain-empowering activities in the treatment of dementia, emphasizing the adaptable nature of the disease and the potential to modify its progression. In the field of dementia care, a notable issue arises from the absence of a comprehensive technical environment that can connect the primary stakeholders involved: dementia patients, family caregivers, trained caregivers, and music therapists. This lack of connectivity presents significant challenges in understanding and monitoring patient behavior, as well as efficiently coordinating and managing therapies.

Trained caregivers, who are often contracted employees, frequently face issues related to their availability for long-term assignments. This inconsistency in their presence makes it difficult for medical staff to access vital information and make informed treatment decisions. Consequently, the continuity of care and the ability to track patient progress become compromised.

Meanwhile, family caregivers, who play a crucial role in supporting individuals with dementia, require access to relevant knowledge and guidance. They need assistance in determining which therapies should be implemented, how to accurately administer these therapies, and how to effectively observe and assess any resulting progress. Without proper resources and support, family caregivers may struggle to make well-informed decisions regarding the best course of action for their loved ones.

Addressing these challenges and bridging the technical gap among stakeholders is essential to improve the quality of dementia care. By establishing a robust and integrated technical infrastructure, it becomes possible to facilitate information sharing, enhance coordination, and empower caregivers with the necessary tools and resources to provide optimal care for dementia patients.

LITERATURE REVIEW

A neurodegenerative disorder called dementia causes behavioral changes, neurological abnormalities, and a steady decline in cognitive function, making it impossible to carry out daily tasks. Effective interventions are required to raise the quality of life for those who have dementia given the rapidly rising prevalence of dementia worldwide. This review of the literature underlines the knowledge gaps that still need to be filled, especially in the Sri Lankan context, and looks at the possible effects of music therapy, physical exercise, and brain-empowering interaction on dementia patients.Globally, dementia is a major public health concern, and by 2050, the number of diagnosed cases is expected to quadruple. Determining modifiable risk factors and creating therapies that can postpone or prevent the onset of dementia are crucial because there is currently no known cure for the disease. As significant modifiable risk factors for dementia, social isolation and physical inactivity have been discovered. This highlights the importance of programs that encourage and enable dementia patients to participate in regular physical activities.According to research, music therapy can benefit those who are living with dementia.

According to studies, listening to tailored music that reflects a person's tastes can reduce the risk of dementia and enhance cognitive function. Listening to music can assist solve small concerns and reduce issues including memory loss, communication abnormalities, melancholy, and anxiety in dementia patients who have musical backgrounds. Music therapy, in contrast to certain other non-pharmacological treatments, is appropriate for people with dementia at all stages.Exercise and mentally challenging activities, such playing games and solving puzzles, have been reported to improve cognitive and functional capacities in people with Alzheimer's disease. Studies show that engaging in cognitively stimulating activities early in the disease's progression is linked to a slower rate of cognitive deterioration. It has been demonstrated that playing board games and engaging in brain-based activities can help prevent dementia and preserve cognitive function.There is a lack of information on music therapy, physical activity engagement, and brain-empowering activities for dementia patients, despite the fact that numerous studies have concentrated on the lack of awareness and understanding of dementia in Sri Lanka.

Additionally, it is also unknown how technology can support caregivers and facilitate these approaches. Connecting dementia patients, family caregivers, trained caregivers, and music therapists would enable coordinated treatment management, progress tracking, and engagement in activities within the Sri Lankan community. This will require a comprehensive and affordable assistive technology solution.There is ample evidence in the literature to support the beneficial effects of music therapy, physical exercise, and brain-enriching engagement on dementia patients. However, more study is required to examine the effectiveness of these interventions in the Sri Lankan setting and create an assistive technology system that may help with engagement, treatment management, and progress monitoring. In order to address the unique needs of dementia patients and carers in Sri Lanka, this literature review identifies the gaps in existing research and underlines the significance of a practical, affordable, and culturally appropriate solution.

Overview diagram

Research Problem

  • How to regulate the daily intake of physical activities among demented individuals.
  • How to intervene music into dementia patient’s life.
  • How to engage dementia patients in brain empowering games.
  • How to track and monitor the overall progress on level of dementia after engaging with above scenarios.
  • How to make predictions based on the progress to determine the sections dementia patient need to invest more time on to improve their cognitive capabilities.

Objectives

Main Objective

  • Developing an assistive tool to support dementia patients and carers in Sri Lanka in conducting physical, music, and brain-empowerment therapy while tracking their progress.

Sub Objectives

  • Ability to personalize therapy recommendations (music, physical exercises, and brain-empowering activity levels) based on user preferences and completion rates
  • Ability to use the application for the to play the games that are implemented.
  • Ability to use the application to track the progress of dementia patients with respect to brain empowering activities.
  • Ability to use the application to play music and provide playlists for the users.
  • Ability to use the application to track the progress of dementia patients with respect to played music.
  • Ability to use mobile application to obtain guidance to conduct physical activities.
  • Ability to use the mobile application to regulate physical activity conduction.
  • Implementation of mobile UIs for care give which are easily check progress of elders.
  • The ability to utilize the application using voice inputs.
  • Implementation of mobile UIs which are easily interactable for elders specially above 60 living in Sri Lanka.
